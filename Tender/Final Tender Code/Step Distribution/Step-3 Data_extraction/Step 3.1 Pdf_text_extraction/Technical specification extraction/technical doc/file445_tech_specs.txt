 It should have Xenon light and fibre optics to enable brilliant view 
of tympanic membrane and ear canal before and during screening. 
 It should be Portable, lightweight air conduction hearing screener. 
 It should take approximately 30-60 seconds to do a full screening 
on both ears 
 It should test three screening levels: 
20dB HL at 500Hz is excellent for school screening 
25dB HL at 1000 or 2000Hz works for most types of patients 
40dB HL at 4000Hz is perfect for age-related hearing loss 
 It should have a built-in 1000 Hz pre-tone that lets patients

 It should be able to emit pure tone signal from 15 to 50 dBHL 
 It should have dedicated headset
